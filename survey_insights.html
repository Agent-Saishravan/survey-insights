Key insights & action areas
🔴
Call load is unsustainable for Shift B

One Shift B member logs 6 post-4PM calls daily with last calls ending at 10:30 PM — a consistent after-hours burden that needs immediate review.

🟠
Adhoc call volume outlier detected

One General shift respondent reported 80 adhoc calls over 3 months (~16/week). This is far above the team average and warrants a direct conversation.

🟣
Recognition is too infrequent

56% want real-time recognition but most currently receive it monthly. Awards and RP Points are the top preferred modes — simple to action immediately.

🔵
GenAI access & training gaps

Multiple respondents want live training, not just documentation. One specifically needs API keys for agentic AI automation workflows.

🟢
Shift allowance coverage confirmed

All 4 Shift B respondents confirmed they receive shift allowance. General and Shift A are correctly marked N/A. No gaps flagged.

💡
Team ideas are immediately actionable

Stand-up restructuring, agenda-first culture, written status updates instead of calls — low cost, high impact changes managers can action now.

Standout ideas from the team

Move side discussions out of stand-ups into separate follow-ups. Scrum Master to steer conversations back on track. Reserve stand-ups for status updates only — not problem-solving.

Simran Kshatriya — General shift · 4+ hrs in calls daily

Send agendas in advance. Set a hard end time when scheduling — people naturally fill whatever time is allotted. Replace status updates with written summaries so calls are reserved for decisions only.

Sathya Murali — General shift · 2–3 hrs in calls daily

Pre-load all data and context before calls so time isn't lost looking things up during the meeting — makes the call noticeably faster and more focused.

Keerthana Chilakala — General shift · 2–3 hrs in calls daily

Organise real-time AI training calls rather than just sharing documentation. Live hands-on sessions work better than static resources for adoption.
